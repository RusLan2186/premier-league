about club

Founded in 1878, Everton Football Club is one of England’s most historic and cherished football institutions. Nicknamed The Toffees, Everton has woven its name deep into the fabric of English football, boasting a rich heritage, passionate fanbase, and a tradition of resilience that has carried the club through highs and lows over nearly a century and a half. Based at the iconic Goodison Park — affectionately known as The Grand Old Lady — Everton has been a cornerstone of the city of Liverpool, not just in footballing terms, but as a symbol of working-class pride, loyalty, and identity. The blue half of Merseyside lives and breathes Everton, with generation after generation of fans passing down the club’s values of heart, hard work, and unwavering support.

Everton trophy cabinet reflects its glorious past: nine First Division (now Premier League) titles, five FA Cups, and a European Cup Winners’ Cup. Legendary players like Dixie Dean — who scored a record 60 goals in a single league season — as well as Alan Ball, Neville Southall, and more recently Tim Cahill and Leighton Baines, have left indelible marks on the club's proud history. Despite recent challenges and a battle to reclaim consistent top-flight form, Everton’s ambition remains clear. With a passionate fanbase behind them, a promising youth academy, and a new stadium on the horizon at Bramley-Moore Dock, the club is gearing up for a new era — one that respects the past but looks boldly toward the future.
